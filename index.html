Sucre enchants visitors with its immaculate whitewashed facades, cobblestone plazas, and living colonial heritage that earned it a place on UNESCO's World Heritage list.

Nestled in an Andean valley at 2,810 metres, Bolivia's constitutional capital seamlessly blends five centuries of Spanish colonial architecture with vibrant indigenous culture. From the very square where Bolivia declared independence in 1825, to the world's largest collection of dinosaur footprints — Sucre is unlike anywhere else on earth.

Travel Guide

Plan Your Visit

Everything you need to know for a seamless trip to Bolivia's most enchanting city.

Getting There

  • ✈ Alcantarí Airport (SRE) — 35 km from centre. Domestic flights from La Paz, Santa Cruz & Cochabamba.
  • 🚌 Overnight bus from La Paz — 12–14 hrs, comfortable cama seats. Several daily departures.
  • 🚕 Airport transfers — shared taxis and minibuses connect the airport with the city centre regularly.

Best Time to Visit

  • ★ April – October (dry season) is ideal — clear blue skies and comfortable daytime temperatures of 18–24°C.
  • ◐ May & June offer the best balance — lush green landscapes just after the rainy season.
  • ☔ Nov – March is wet season — fewer tourists, lower prices, but afternoon rains are common.

Good to Know

  • 💰 Currency: Boliviano (BOB) — ~6.9 BOB = 1 USD. Sucre is very affordable for western visitors.
  • 🌡️ Altitude: 2,810 m — allow 1–2 days to acclimatise. Drink plenty of water and sip coca tea.
  • 🗣️ Language: Spanish (one of Latin America's clearest accents). Quechua is also spoken locally.
